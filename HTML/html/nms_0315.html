From: Editor, The National Midnight Star 
Subject: Administrivia

First of all, there are two new files at the ftp site.  They are:

    dreamline.voc   and    dreamline.sb

The first is a Soundblaster VOC file, and the second is another Soundblaster
compatable file.  Interesting, the .sb file is the same size as the .au file!
Ken Renard provided them, but says credit Lance Norskog (thinman@netcom.com).
Thanks to both!

I've also been informed that you can play the "dreamline.au" file on a Sun
using soundtool in Sunview or x_soundtool in X windows.

Secondly, seeing as how only a few lucky ones have gotten copies of the new
album, or even heard songs on the radio, it might be a Nice Thing (tm) if we
toned down discussions of them until they're more widely available.  I mean,
it's kind of cruel to say "it's an excellent song" if 80% of the folks around
can't find out for a month.  I dunno, call me what you will, it was just a
thought...  As usual, I won't prevent you, but your karma might suffer in the
long run.  :-)

Date: Wed, 14 Aug 91 20:52:48 -0700
From: wbarry@cory.berkeley.edu (Bill Barry)
Subject: The Bay Area Dreamline wait

Hello all, Here's some info on the reason why us Bay Area fans haven't heard
Dreamline. I just got off the phone with KOME. The DJ said that the stations
over on the east coast , or the rest of country, are playing Dreamline and
other songs illegally. He would not tell me for sure if he had a copy of the
single at the station. However, he said they have not got authorization
from Atlantic to play it even if they did. He went on to say that the station
has a very good relationship with Rush, ie they are always happy to do inter-
views with them and have been good to them in the past. He told me that if
they were to play this single illegally then it would be a real slap in the
face to the band and they just would not do that because it wouldn't be fair.
Anyway, he didn't have an exact release date for playing the single though
he expected  to get it by next week. So hope this may clear things up!

Also, has anybody heard about any videos for the new songs? I know this
sounds kind of early but I remember that the video for The Big MOney
was played on MTV before the album was officially released.

Until later....

Bill Barry

comes from all three.  but "emotion" can mean different things to everybody, so
i'm probably very wrong in the eyes of reviewers or people majoring in music
theory or most of the people in music today...

anyway, this is what i think about when the boss-man's out of town and it's my
last day of work...

From: jaeger@buphy.bu.edu (Gregg Jaeger)
Subject: The Thing explained?

Hi Everybody,

I liked reading Neil's 'post' the other day (thanks to the one who
provided it!!!). His comments on `Where's My Thing?'' jogged my
memory to provide a possible `explanation' of the title. His
statement was it is derived from a situation where one just
can't think of the word one's looking for to describe something.
I recalled a recording I have (somewhere! I can't find it in all
my stuff -- it may or may not be (though I think it is) an
official Rush release recording -- where Geddy's talking to the
audience of a concert and he's introducing `La Villa' and instead
of saying 'This is Mr. Alex Lifeson on acoustic guitar -- this
is La Villa Strangiato' he says (paraphrase) `Here's Alex on
the thing... the thing... This is La Villa Strangiato.' So
we have Geddy saying `the thing' while introducing an instrumental.
Hmmm.... Maybe this incident became an amusement associated with
an instrumental? I can easily imaging Alex coming back with
`Where's my thing?' pretending he can't find his guitar.

Just a guess!

Gregg
