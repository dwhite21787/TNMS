what, 38 years young?

between yesterday around 16:00 until about 9:00 this morning (EDT).

them send me mail containing their name and an e-mail address at either
address in my .sig, and I'll put 'em right on.  I try for next-day service,
but can't always guarantee it, especially over the weekend.

Date: Mon, 26 Aug 91 14:10:12 PDT
From: tspalmer@ocfmail.ocf.llnl.gov (Todd Palmer)
Subject: Bay Area Roll the Bones Premiere and Other Stuff

Hi, yall:

I called around to the stations in the Bay Area
that I can receive (KRQR, KOME, KSJO, KZAP) and
asked about the radio premiere of Roll the Bones
and would they be carrying it?  The responses were
uninspiring, in general, but KZAP (a station in
Sacramento that carries a great deal of premiere shows)
said that they probably would carry it.  KOME said they
would check on it and let the us all know later (not too
much later I hope...)

Also, I'd like to raise an issue that has bothered me for some time.
If any of us true fans were to get the great honor of meeting
the Three Canucks, what would you say to them?  I have often
thought about this because the tendency is to say stuff like
"Gee, I think you guys are really great and I have liked your
music since before I was born", or whatever.  I hope that none
of us would want to come across like that.  But the question is
how do communicate with people that you greatly admire and respect,
so as to distinguish yourself as someone that they can relate to
on a personal level, not the typical musician-to-fan level.  I
realize that these guys are not probably going to take the instant
"Oh my god, you are THE GUY... You have exactly the qualities we are
looking for in a 'best' friend.  Would you like to come back to
Canada and meet our families... " attitude, but you would like to
leave them with the impression that you are not just some stupid
hero-worshipper with nothing to say.  So, what do you think?
Have any of you out there actually met them?  What were your
impressions?

Todd "Palmsation" Palmer

Date: Mon, 26 Aug 91 15:56:46 HST
From: hinano@ahi.pbrc.Hawaii.Edu (Hinano Akaka)
Subject: Dreamline/Boxed Set

Yo.

Well, the local station has been playing only "Dreamline".  I
requested "Where's My Thing" only to be answered by "Play what?".
Guess I'll have to keep trying.  Auugghhh!  This is so aggravating!!

If no one objects, I could certainly try my hand at "unofficially"
transcribing the lyrics to "Dreamline" -- I've only had the song stuck
in  my head for a week...

For anyone and everyone interested, hopefully we'll get some news
first-hand on whether they'll put out a boxed set.  I asked Neil via
the Rush Backstage Club about whether they'll put one on.  (Along with
the 400 other  questions I asked him)  Whenever he decides to answer
all the questions the rest of the world has been sending him, hopefuly
he'll answer that one.

Oh yeah, I also asked him about why POW and P were brought to us by
letters.  Actually, this was partly a real question and partly a joke.
He complained the last time that the questions were too serious, so I
hope he takes it as a joke...

And now for something completely different...  Personally, I think the
folks on this Digest have every right to talk ad nauseum about the new
songs/album  UNLESS the same person, or a whole lot of people,
regurgitate the same exact info.  I mean, face it, we've been waiting,
quite patiently, too, for this album to come out since DECEMBER --
we're all beyond the point of excitement and this is the only outlet
we have to express our repressed joy.  Especially since, ideally,
we're the only ones who understand what it's like for Rush to come out
with new material.  And if we can't rely on each other for support and
understanding of our ecstacy, then why bother having this digest?  Let
people be excited and ecstatic  and freak out -- THAT'S PART OF THE
FUN!!!

ROLL THE BONES, MAN!!!!!

puanani akaka

Subject: it's me again
Date: Mon, 26 Aug 91 22:44:27 EDT

Yes, I know I posted earlier and this is not Rush-related but last Wednesday
was a "special day".  Not only was new Rush played in this area but I saw
Van Halen at the Blossom Music Center that night.  EXCELLENT show.  For those
who have a chance to see them - GO!  I was quite disappointed when I saw them
on the _1984_ tour but things are different now.  Not cause of Hagar but the
music (and them) have matured somewhat (at least that's what I think).  And
the new album rocks your face off.  It's great to hear good 'ol fasion R 'n' R
again (and especially from them).  Also, the show was musically sound (which
is good cause they are only in the 2nd week or so of touring).  Like I said,
if you can see them - do so.  Eddie's guitar solo alone is worth the $$.

We now return you to your regularly scheduled Rush News ...

Dan...

Date: Tue, 27 Aug 91 11:12:00 -0400
From: at656@cleveland.Freenet.Edu (Michael Sensor)
Subject: RTB and Philly Radio

Hello folks,

	Just got back into the swing of things here with my first issue
of TNMS after the summer.  Gosh, I can't believe how much the topics of
discussion have changed since May.  Now, instead of hearing vague rumors
about a new album (entitled "Throw the Stones"or "Rolling Stone Can Smoke
My Bone", &c.), we're reading definite stuff. I like it!

	But, I digress.  I'm writing to express my amazement that people have
actually *heard* more tracks from RTB than "Dreamline".  Here In Philly, the
only semi-good radio station is WMMR, which had to be tortured endlessly by
my girlfriend and I before they would play "DL".  They premiered it last
Wednesday and have played it only rarely since.  Needless to say, we haven't
heard hide nor hair of any of the other tracks.

	Are we listening to the wrong station or what? Seems that Southeastern
Pennsylvania suffers from a dearth of good radio.

[ When I'm in Philly, I listen to 93.3 and 94.1; one isn't WMMR. :-)
                                                                  :rush-mgr ]

	Regarding the radio premiere of RTB: What's going on? Some say it's
a live set (like the premiere of FBN at Electric Ladyland in 1974 (which I
have, if anyone's interested)), and some claim that it's a satellite 'cast.
If the latter is true, will Westwood One carry it? Help!!

	Finally, for those Rush-fans in the Erie, PA area (my hometown),
Rocket 101 (WRKT 100.9) will premiere this album in some form on the 29th
at 11 along with every other station on the planet.  They have been playing
"DL" quite often...

	Keep rocking, you Rushians, and have pity on those of us who must
wait until the 29th! :-) :-)

	Mike

Subject: More HYF symbols

I was reading the FAQL last night and there was a question about the HYF
symbols on the inside cover.  I had a little bit of time to kill last night
so I dragged out my CD, Album, and tour book and found a few more -

1.  Leaning against the trash can in the front is an oxford shoe, just like
    the one the girl wears on the ESL cover and Tom Sawyer video.

2.  The back side of the owl on FBN is resembled on the lamp post on the far
    right side.  (Only on the CD and tour book)

3.  There is a woman looking out the window next to the Statue of Liberty.

4.  A copy of the painting on the far left on MP, is wrapped up in cloth and
    leaning against the front steps.

That's all for now.  If I find myself waiting in the E.R. waiting room again
I will probably look for some more hidden symbols.  (everybody's fine)
