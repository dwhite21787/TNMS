From: Editor, The National Midnight Star 
Subject: Administrivia

Ok, folks, here we are, back on the air.  It's been a long time, and I
apoligise, but I hope we can re-start the regularity of the NMS now.
This issue will be the current/latest mail, and other issues will come
out in the next few days to catch up on the backlogged mail (around 120
items).  There will be two mailings per day until this is caught up.  I'm
sorry if some of the time-critical stuff missed the mark...

I will NOT be moving mail with spoiler information to the ends of the
digests; posters please put "SPOILER" in your subject if you post about
a show you've seen.

There are a bunch of new things:  new tabs in the ftp tab directory (take
a look), plus an updated tab creation program.  There are also some new
images, too many to detail here.  Check the images directory - the CONTENTS
file is updated.  A file containing the song spoofs is available in the 
'special' directory under the name "song.parodies".  It contains songs from
the current album and a couple of old ones.  The headers were left intact
so we can credit (blame?) the authors.

Well, that's all for now.  Hopefully this is the start of the regular
deliveries again!

rush-mgr

From: rlr@bbt.com (Ron Rader)
Subject: Pushead
Date: Wed, 6 Nov 91 17:12:25 EST

Ross asks:

> Imagine my shock to notice that my Black RTB t-shirt's graphics were drawn by
> the one and only Pushead! (of Mettalica artwork fame) Has Pushead done other
> (major) contributions to bands, or is this his first one he's done outside of
> the Mighty Met?

  Pushead has done loads and loads of work in the hardcore scene.  Many old
fanzines are graced with his tender pens and brushes.  Pus' distinctive
style sticks out from the usual boring and/or campy-shocking music artwork.

  Pushead did covers for a number of albums.  The one that immediately comes
to mind is Corrosion Of Conformity's _Animosity_ (their 2nd release).  CoC
is a hardcore (kinda' speed metal) band out of Raleigh, NC.  They are a
mighty mighty band, with a brand new release (whose name I forget; with
only 2 original band members though).  You can pick up _Animosity_ and
the new one fairly easily.  Well worth it.

  Ron "CoC Head" Rader

Date: Thu, 7 Nov 91 15:43:19 -0500
From: darren@bucsf.bu.edu (Darren Chamberlain)
Subject: Re: Rush RollTheBones Tour Shirt

Ross Allen Roberts writes:
>Imagine my shock to notice that my Black RTB t-shirt's graphics were drawn by
>the one and only Pushead! (of Mettalica artwork fame) Has Pushead done other
>(major) contributions to bands, or is this his forst one he's done outside of
>the Mightly Met?

Pushead did the cover art for Prong's _Beg_to_Differ_, and has been doing
work underground bands for about ten years in California.  He was asked to
do the artwork for Anthrax's _Persistence_of_Time_ but either refused or
wasn't allowed to by Brockum, the company that releases all his published
artwork.

for sixth-row (yep, sixth-row) seats. (Got 'em over the phone, too.)

I wasn't that lucky this time. The phone lines were tied up forever, and I
had to call a ticket agency. I got second-level seats for $25 a pop.
Most people cannot afford tickets going for $30 and up. Who can I complain
to?

A second show has been added in NYC, Dec. 7 at Madison Square Garden (the first
show completely sold out!)

Has anyone seen the RTB video on eMpTyV? I hear it's really good!
I have a hunch Atlantic will release a video compliation shortly.

That's about it for now. Donna S., are you still hosting a Rush party the
weekend before the show? Rush-mgr, will you be driving up the interstate
to see the Philly show?

Keep 'em rolling...

Subject: !!!RTB Video Was Actually On MTV!!!
From: vinnie@darkside.com (Vindicator)
Date: Mon, 11 Nov 91 20:32:04 PST

Yup it's true, the video was finally on and I taped it.

I was house sitting for the weekend and the people I was sitting for
happen to have MTV.  So I was taping on 6 hour shifts hoping to get it.
For two days I was scanning the tapes only to find this 2ligit crap and a
bunch of other nonsense.  Then it happened...

As I was fast forwarding through a Tom Petty video, I looked down at the
TV Guide, and when I looked up... lo and behold,  there they were rockin
infront of the wall of dice.

I was suprised because they played the entire thing from beginning to
end.  Here is a brief synopsis,  if you don't want to see this just cover
your eyes or something.

The video starts out showing some album cover posters in a window, it
then shows a closeup where the kid on the cover comes to life.  We then
get to see gedster and those other two cool guys.  Neil has his new
drums, and yes geddy's hair is down, like the photo in Guitar Player.

They are playing before the wall of dice and it cuts between them and the
kid infront of the wall.  Soon the kid falls and breaks down a part of
the wall exposing the blue sky and other killer things.

The solo takes place with Alex in a sorta snowstorm of dice (way cool)
with some distant shots of him a geddy, you can only see their siloettes
infront of a bright light.  The rapper is a nifty skeleton, not really
animated but more like a marionette.  Very funny,  mohawk hair and all.

Neil sports his colorfull bandana and plays with energy, some coll shots
of him.  Overall, probibly my most favorite video of all time.  I have
watched it so much I think the quality of my tape in faultering.  If
anyone wants a copy please contact me via e-mail.   vinnie@darkside.com

The only rub to the whole thing.  I calculated when it was played on MTV
according to my tape.  Not during the day.  Not primetime.  Nope, at 1 in
the morning PST, that means 3 or 4 on the east coast assuming there is no
delay.  Hey they played it,  but in great MTV fashion, they played it at
a very cheesy time.

After you see it answer this one question.  Who wouldn't like this video?

        Dan-

Well maybe some Finlanders....  but that's about it!\
